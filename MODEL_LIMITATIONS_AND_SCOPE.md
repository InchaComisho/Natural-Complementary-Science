# Model Limitations and Scope

## Overview

This document defines the limitations, boundaries, and appropriate use of the Natural Complementary Science framework and its associated documents and systems.

Reading this document before drawing conclusions from the framework is strongly recommended.

---

## 1. Nature of This Framework

Natural Complementary Science is, at its current stage of development, a **conceptual, philosophical, and integrative framework**.

It is:

```text
[+] A structural paradigm for understanding the relationship between humanity and nature
[+] A philosophical reorientation of the foundational assumptions of science and civilization design
[+] An organizing framework that integrates existing scientific knowledge under six principles
[+] A hypothesis-generating system identifying structural gaps in conventional approaches
[+] A long-term civilizational vision, not a short-term policy toolkit
```

It is **not**:

```text
[-] A peer-reviewed scientific theory
[-] An empirically validated predictive model
[-] A policy document with specific prescriptions for governments or institutions
[-] A replacement for IPCC Assessment Reports or established climate science
[-] A claim of resolved scientific certainty on any mechanism described
```

## 3. Carbon Fixation Collapse Hypothesis

A central premise of this framework is that atmospheric CO₂ accumulation is driven not only by emissions, but by the collapse of carbon fixation systems (terrestrial, oceanic, and soil-based).

### Validated Components

- Ongoing deforestation reducing terrestrial carbon uptake: **supported by measurement**
- Ocean surface warming reducing CO₂ solubility: **established physics**
- Soil degradation reducing soil carbon stocks: **supported by soil science literature**
- Wetland loss reducing peat carbon storage: **documented and measured**

### Unvalidated Components

- The aggregate magnitude of carbon fixation collapse relative to emission increases: **active scientific debate**
- Whether fixation collapse is a "primary driver" comparable in magnitude to emissions: **not established in mainstream literature**
- Specific rates of fixation recovery under restoration scenarios: **uncertain; model-dependent**

The hypothesis should be treated as a **research direction and structural inquiry**, not an established scientific finding.

## 4. On Decarbonization

Natural Complementary Science explicitly states its position on decarbonization:

```text
This framework does NOT deny that CO₂ is a greenhouse gas.
This framework does NOT deny that human emissions have caused global warming.
This framework does NOT oppose decarbonization.
```

What this framework argues:

```text
Decarbonization is necessary.
Decarbonization alone is not sufficient.

Atmospheric CO₂ stabilization requires:
1. Emission reduction (decarbonization)
2. Carbon fixation restoration (terrestrial, oceanic, soil)
3. Thermal balance management (addressing accumulated ocean heat)

Without all three, the carbon equation cannot be brought into balance.
```

This is a structural argument, not a denial of climate science.  
The specific claims about the magnitude of the insufficiency require empirical validation.

## 5. On Temperature and Thermal Inertia

The framework discusses thermal inertia and baseline temperature rise.

Established mainstream climate science holds:
- Ocean thermal inertia means temperatures continue rising for decades after emissions stabilize
- Full thermal equilibration takes centuries
- Ocean heat content increase is measured and documented

The specific parameter values used in associated models (e.g., `baseline_temperature_rise`, `ocean_heat_inertia`) are **hypothetical constructs for structural reasoning**, not empirically calibrated measurements.

## 6. On Complementary Technology Interventions

The framework proposes that technology aligned with natural law can function as a complementary restoration instrument.

This is a **philosophical and design principle**, not a blanket endorsement of any specific technology.

Evaluation criteria for any proposed complementary technology:

```text
[Required for consideration as "complementary"]
→ Designed to restore a natural process, not replace it permanently
→ Designed to be withdrawn when natural self-regulation resumes
→ Does not create new systemic dependencies that prevent natural recovery
→ Does not produce waste streams that natural systems cannot absorb
→ Aligned with natural law (physics, thermodynamics, ecology)

[Not sufficient without empirical validation]
→ Conceptual alignment with principles
→ Small-scale proof of concept
→ Computer model support alone
```

No technology described in this framework has passed all validation criteria.  
All proposed technologies remain at conceptual or early development stage.

## 7. What This Framework Does Not Claim

To prevent misuse, the following explicit disavowals:

```text
[Not claimed]
This framework does NOT claim to provide a validated scientific prediction
of Earth's future climate trajectory.

This framework does NOT claim that any specific technology
will reverse global warming.

This framework does NOT claim that implementing these systems
is currently feasible at required scale.

This framework does NOT claim to replace the scientific consensus
on climate change, carbon cycle dynamics, or ecosystem function.

This framework does NOT assign a timeline to any outcome.

This framework does NOT claim to be policy-ready
without independent scientific and engineering review.
```

## 8. Appropriate Use

### Appropriate

```text
[+] As a structural and philosophical framework for expanding climate strategy discourse
[+] As a hypothesis-generating framework for scientific investigation
[+] As a design philosophy for technology development (subject to independent validation)
[+] As a framework for civilizational visioning and long-term planning exploration
[+] As an intellectual record of a structural critique of conventional approaches
[+] As a starting point for interdisciplinary research
```

### Not Appropriate Without Further Validation

```text
[-] As the basis for specific policy decisions without independent scientific review
[-] As evidence that specific technologies will work as described
[-] As a substitute for peer-reviewed climate science or engineering analysis
[-] As a basis for dismissing or deprioritizing decarbonization efforts
[-] As a claim of established scientific fact on any mechanism described
```

## 9. Epistemic Humility Statement

Natural Complementary Science emerges from the observation that existing frameworks are structurally incomplete.

This does not mean Natural Complementary Science is complete.

Any framework that claims to resolve the complexity of Earth's systems and humanity's relationship to them should be treated with skepticism — including this one.

The appropriate stance:

```text
Use this framework to ask better questions.
Do not use it to claim final answers.
```
