# Core Definition: Natural Complementary Science

## What Is Natural Complementary Science?

Natural Complementary Science (自然補完科学) is a scientific and philosophical framework that redefines the relationship between human civilization and the natural world on the basis of **complementarity** — a bidirectional, equal relationship in which each party supplies what the other lacks.

It was defined and first published by Master (inchacomisho / inchacomusho) in January–February 2026.

Natural Complementary Science is **not**:
- A branch of complementary medicine or alternative therapy
- A synonym for environmentalism, conservation biology, or sustainability science
- An anti-technology or anti-civilization stance
- A passive preservation ideology

Natural Complementary Science **is**:
- A paradigm-level framework for integrating natural law into science, technology, and civilization design
- A structural reorientation of how humanity understands its role within Earth's systems
- A conceptual and philosophical framework (currently unvalidated as a complete scientific theory)
- An attempt to identify what is structurally missing from both conventional science and conventional environmentalism

## Why "Complementarity" and Not "Coexistence" or "Symbiosis"?

### The Problem with "Coexistence"

The phrase *coexistence with nature* is widely used but structurally ambiguous.

In biology, coexistence and symbiosis encompass a spectrum of relationships including mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism. Coexistence does not inherently imply equality or mutual benefit.

In common usage, "coexisting with nature" often describes human activity that is less destructive than before — but still fundamentally extractive and anthropocentric. The underlying logic remains:

```text
Nature exists to provide resources for humanity.
We should manage those resources wisely.
```

This is resource management with ethical framing — not a genuinely equal relationship.

### The Meaning of Complementarity

Complementarity describes a relationship in which two systems **mutually compensate for what the other lacks**, forming a more complete and stable whole.

Applied to the human-nature relationship:

```text
Humanity depends on nature for survival:
→ oxygen, water, food, climate regulation, biological cycles

Nature, having been severely disrupted by human activity,
now depends on humanity for recovery:
→ ecosystem reconstruction, soil restoration, ocean circulation support,
  climate stabilization, microbiome regeneration
```

This bidirectionality is the basis of equality.

Neither party is servant to the other.  
Neither party is superior to the other.  
Each party is responsible to the other.

This is the precise meaning embedded in the term "Natural Complementary Science."

## The Equal Complementary Relationship Between Humanity and Nature

### Three Rejected Models

Natural Complementary Science explicitly rejects three historically dominant models:

| Model | Description | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| **Domination** | Nature is a resource to be controlled and optimized | Destroys the systems humanity depends on |
| **Paternalistic protection** | Nature must be preserved in its current state by human management | Treats nature as fragile object, not dynamic partner |
| **Passive non-intervention** | Humans should step back and let nature self-heal | Ignores that many systems are too damaged to self-repair within relevant timescales |

### The Complementary Model

```text
Equal Complementary Relationship:

Humanity ←→ Nature

Humanity provides:
- Intentional intervention where natural self-repair has broken down
- Restoration of circulation, soil, and water systems
- Long-term systemic stewardship

Nature provides:
- Carbon fixation and oxygen generation
- Water cycling, climate regulation
- Biological diversity and systemic resilience
- The foundational conditions for human life
```

In this model, human technology is not inherently destructive.  
Technology aligned with natural law can function as a **complementary instrument** — extending and restoring natural processes that have been disrupted.

## Relationship to Existing Science: Integration, Not Rejection

Natural Complementary Science does not reject conventional scientific disciplines.

It does not deny:
- Physics, chemistry, biology, ecology
- The greenhouse gas mechanism of CO₂
- The scientific findings of climate science
- The value of existing environmental and conservation science

What it proposes is a **reorientation of the framework** within which these disciplines operate.

```text
Conventional science:
→ Fragmented disciplines
→ Nature as external object
→ Human optimization as goal

Natural Complementary Science:
→ Integrated system-level framework
→ Nature as co-constitutive partner
→ Systemic continuity as goal
```

The six foundational principles (Natural Law, Harmony, Circulation, Structure, Order, Wa) provide the integrating framework through which existing scientific knowledge is reorganized and reapplied.

This is not a replacement of science.  
It is a reorganization of science under a different foundational assumption about humanity's place in natural systems.

## Neither Domination Nor Abandonment

The critical philosophical position of Natural Complementary Science:

```text
The Earth's natural systems have been damaged beyond the point
where passive non-intervention is sufficient.

But the response to damaged systems is not more domination.

The response is complementary restoration:
- Targeted, law-aligned intervention
- Designed to restore natural processes, not replace them
- Withdrawn when natural self-regulation resumes
```

This is analogous to medical intervention in a damaged organism:
- A physician does not control the body's healing
- A physician creates conditions for healing to occur
- The body's own processes complete the recovery

Natural Complementary Science proposes humanity take this role at a planetary scale.

## Summary Definition

```text
Natural Complementary Science is a scientific and philosophical framework
that establishes an equal, bidirectional complementary relationship
between human civilization and the natural world,
grounded in natural law and six foundational principles,
aimed at restoring Earth's systemic continuity
through law-aligned human intervention
where natural self-repair has broken down.
```
