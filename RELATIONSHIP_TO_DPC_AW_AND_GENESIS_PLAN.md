## 1. Direct Planetary Cooling (地球直接冷却)

### What It Is

Direct Planetary Cooling refers to a set of physical interventions aimed at directly addressing:
- Atmospheric heat accumulation (thermal inertia)
- Ocean surface temperature elevation
- Disrupted ocean biological metabolism
- Weakened carbon fixation capacity

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

Natural Complementary Science provides the **foundational rationale** for why Direct Planetary Cooling is necessary.

The argument chain:

```text
Natural Complementary Science establishes:
→ Earth's natural thermal and carbon circulation systems have been damaged
→ Passive non-intervention is insufficient (systems cannot self-repair within relevant timescales)
→ Complementary intervention — aligned with natural law — is required

Direct Planetary Cooling operationalizes this:
→ Physical mechanisms to support ocean metabolism, heat distribution, and carbon fixation
→ Designed to restore natural processes, not replace them
→ Intended to be withdrawn as natural self-regulation resumes
```

Direct Planetary Cooling is Natural Complementary Science applied to the **thermal and carbon dimension** of Earth's system.

## 2. OBS: Ocean Biological System (海洋生体系)

### What It Is

OBS refers to interventions that support ocean biological system function:
- Phytoplankton productivity support
- Biological carbon pump restoration
- Marine food web circulation support
- Reduction of ocean surface stratification effects on nutrient availability

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

OBS is the application of the Circulation and Structure principles to the ocean dimension:

```text
Circulation principle:
→ The ocean biological carbon pump is a critical component of global carbon circulation
→ Stratification disrupts vertical nutrient flow, breaking circulation
→ OBS interventions restore the conditions for vertical biological circulation

Structure principle:
→ The trophic structure of marine ecosystems depends on phytoplankton as foundation
→ Restoring phytoplankton productivity supports the structural integrity of marine systems
```

OBS is complementary restoration of ocean biological circulation — not replacement of it.

## 3. OTU: Ocean Tuning Unit (海洋調律ユニット)

### What It Is

OTU is a proposed physical system for facilitating deep-water upwelling:
- Bringing cold, nutrient-rich deep water to the surface
- Providing direct thermal cooling effect on surface waters
- Supplying nutrients to support phytoplankton

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

OTU operationalizes the Natural Law and Order principles:

```text
Natural Law principle:
→ Ocean thermohaline circulation is the natural mechanism for vertical water transport
→ Warming disrupts this circulation (reduced density differential)
→ OTU seeks to support the circulation pattern within physical law, not override it

Order principle:
→ Thermal stratification represents disrupted natural order in the ocean column
→ OTU aims to partially restore the thermal and chemical gradients
   that support natural biological ordering
```

OTU is explicitly hypothetical and requires engineering validation.  
The concept is grounded in existing oceanographic principles but has not been validated at scale.

## 4. UMC: Urban Metabolic Circulation (都市代謝循環)

### What It Is

UMC refers to the redesign of urban systems to function as metabolically integrated nodes within natural circulation:
- Closing organic material loops (food waste → humus → soil → food)
- Reducing urban heat island effects
- Reintegrating urban water cycles with natural watershed systems
- Converting urban areas from net carbon emitters to potential carbon-neutral or carbon-sequestering nodes

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

UMC is the application of the Circulation principle to the urban-civilizational dimension:

```text
Conventional city:
→ Linear metabolism: import resources → consume → export waste
→ Thermal and material isolation from surrounding natural systems
→ Net atmospheric heat and CO₂ emitter

UMC-designed city:
→ Closed material loops: organic waste becomes soil input, not landfill
→ Urban vegetation and water features moderate thermal load
→ Urban systems function as nodes within, not barriers to, watershed and ecosystem function
```

Urban systems cover a small fraction of Earth's surface but currently dominate thermal and material flows.  
UMC proposes that this influence could be redirected toward complementary restoration.

## 5. Humus Recycling / Organic Circulation (腐葉土循環)

### What It Is

Humus Recycling refers to the systematic return of organic matter — food waste, leaf litter, agricultural residues — to soil as humus, rebuilding soil carbon and microbial health.

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

Humus recycling is the most direct application of the Circulation principle:

```text
Natural carbon circulation:
Photosynthesis → biomass → decomposition → humus → soil carbon → plant nutrition → photosynthesis

Industrial disruption:
Biomass → food waste → landfill (linear, not circular)
→ Methane emission in anaerobic decomposition
→ Soil carbon depletion from lack of organic return

Humus recycling restores:
→ The decomposition → humus → soil carbon link
→ Soil microbial community support
→ Long-term terrestrial carbon sequestration
```

Humus recycling requires no advanced technology.  
It is the restoration of a circulation that functioned for billions of years before industrial disruption.

## 6. Desert Greening (砂漠緑地化)

### What It Is

Desert Greening refers to the restoration of vegetation cover, soil structure, and water retention in degraded dryland ecosystems.

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

Desert Greening applies the Harmony and Circulation principles to degraded land:

```text
Harmony principle:
→ Arid and semi-arid zones naturally support diverse dryland communities
→ Many deserts are degraded grasslands or savanna systems — not climatically inevitable
→ Restoring functional diversity to degraded dryland systems restores their role in carbon and water cycling

Circulation principle:
→ Vegetated soil retains water, feeds microbial communities, and captures atmospheric carbon
→ Bare degraded soil loses water through surface runoff, erodes, and releases carbon
→ Desert greening reconnects degraded areas to global water and carbon circuits
```

Desert greening also has implications for regional climate stabilization through albedo change and evapotranspiration effects — though these require validation at scale.

## 7. Artificial Wisdom (人工叡智)

### What It Is

Artificial Wisdom (AW) is a proposed framework for artificial intelligence that operates not only on efficiency and task optimization, but on **alignment with natural law, Wa, and long-term systemic continuity**.

It is distinguished from conventional AI by its value foundation:
- Conventional AI: optimize specified objective functions
- Artificial Wisdom: evaluate actions against natural law, systemic health, and civilizational continuity

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

Natural Complementary Science provides the value framework that Artificial Wisdom must be aligned with:

```text
Natural Complementary Science defines:
→ What constitutes alignment with natural law
→ What systemic health means across six principles
→ What the equal complementary relationship between humanity and nature requires

Artificial Wisdom applies:
→ These values in real-time decision support
→ Across the complex, interdependent interventions of Direct Planetary Cooling, UMC, OBS, etc.
→ At the scale and speed that human judgment alone cannot maintain
```

Without a coherent value framework, AI optimization tends toward short-term efficiency at the cost of long-term systemic health.  
Natural Complementary Science provides the framework.  
Artificial Wisdom is the implementation of that framework in computational form.

## 8. Wa-Node (和ノード)

### What It Is

Wa-Node is the proposed implementation unit of Artificial Wisdom — a node in a distributed network of AI systems that evaluate decisions and actions against the Wa principle and the broader Natural Complementary Science framework.

### Relationship to Natural Complementary Science

Wa-Node operationalizes the Wa principle at the network level:

```text
Single optimizing AI → may maximize local objective at systemic cost
Wa-Node network → evaluates each action against whole-system coherence
                   across natural law, harmony, circulation, structure, order, and Wa
```

Wa-Nodes are proposed as the judgment layer of the entire integrated system — ensuring that interventions from DPC, OBS, OTU, UMC, and Desert Greening remain aligned with the complementary relationship rather than drifting toward new forms of domination.
